Then the expression is broken into three separate groups. Group 1 ([a-z0-9_\.-]+) - In this section of the expression, we match one or more lowercase letters between a-z, numbers between 0-9, underscores, periods, and hyphens. The expression is then followed by an @ sign. Group 2 ([\da-z\.-]+) - Next, the domain name must be matched which can use one or more digits, letters between a-z, periods, and hyphens. The domain name is then followed by a period \.. Group 3 ([a-z\.]{2,5}) - Lastly, the third group matches the top level domain. This section looks for any group of letters or dots that are 2-5 characters long. This can also account for region-specific top level domains.
